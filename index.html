Avishek Nag

Associate Professor · School of Computer Science

University College Dublin, Ireland

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Computer Science at University College Dublin. I received my BE (Hons) from Jadavpur University (2005), MTech from IIT Kharagpur (2007), and PhD from the University of California, Davis (2012). I have authored over 100 publications across journals, conference proceedings, and book chapters.

My research interests include cross-layer optimisation in wired and wireless networks, network reliability, mathematics of networks (optimisation, graph theory), network virtualisation, software-defined networks, machine learning, data analytics, and quantum communication. I am a Senior Member of IEEE. I am currently supervising several PhD students and welcome enquiries from prospective researchers.

  • QUADRATURE: Scalable Multi-Chip Quantum Architectures Enabled by Cryogenic Wireless / Quantum-Coherent Network-in-Package Active

    Horizon Europe · €216,028 · 2023–2027

    Addresses the fundamental challenge of scaling quantum computing architectures by developing cryogenic wireless and quantum-coherent network-in-package technologies to connect multiple quantum processor chips.

  • ATLANTIC-eVISION: Cross-Atlantic Experimental Validation of Intelligent SDN-controlled IoT Networks Completed

    Horizon 2020 · €57,128 · 2021–2022

    Validated intelligent, software-defined networking approaches for IoT across cross-Atlantic EU–US experimental testbeds, demonstrating real-world interoperability and network performance.
